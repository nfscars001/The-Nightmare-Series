About the artist

Wei Ho a.k.a Jacky Ho

An artist born and raised in Taiwan, who has been influenced by his American sci-fi movies and TV shows since age 5, his parents always wanted him to become a teacher, lawyer, or doctor, you name it. But this kid never followed the rules, which made his parents and teachers a headache sometimes. Years after, he became a fashion designer and also teaches at universities and colleges. At least he came with a textile engineer background; he is still an engineer, right?
Remember, he never followed the rules, as did his designs and artworks. He likes to combine a lot of different mediums with some science—his painting combined with fabric, coffee ground, 3D printing technology…etc., The most important thing is most of his artwork comes as a collection with a story behind it, and this is how it makes his art more interesting. 

THE NIGHTMARE SERIES

2021~2022
Concept and story behind
The Nightmare Series has two parts, each based on a different universe and style, but the inspiration is based on the nightmare I had as a kid.
When I created these pieces, I studied a lot of psychology to make the audience feel uncomfortable or scared when they see them.

The Guardian

Year: 2022
Size: ​11x17 inch
Media: ​Digital watercolor and pencil sketch

I’m an outdoors person; I love hiking in the mountains, so I love plants and the forest, but I also got scared in the woods because of the horror movie I watched when I was a kid “The Guardian (1990), directed by William Friedkin”

This piece was inspired by one thing I love and I am scared about.
